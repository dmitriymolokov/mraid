"Seraphina!"
"Mom?" My throat was raw, I jolted awake in bed. She hadn't reached out in ten years-not unless it was the worst kind of news.
Her voice trembled through the phone, sharp and brittle.
"Your father-" Her breath hitched, then broke. "He's been attacked."
My stomach clenched. Ice-cold fear gripped me.
"What?!"
"Oh, Sera, he's barely clinging to life!" my mother sobbed brokenly.
I immediately threw the covers off me and jumped out of bed.
"Send me the hospital address," I said in a shaky voice. "I'll be there as soon as I can."
I tried not to make too much noise as I rushed down the stairs so I wouldn't wake my son, Daniel. 
The light underneath my husband, Kieran's, office told me he was still up. As Alpha of the pack, he always had too much to handle.
And if I were honest with myself-too much resentment toward me.
